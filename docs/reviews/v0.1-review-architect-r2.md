## New architectural concerns

### N1 — "1.0" vs "1.0.0" is a small but durable choice

**Severity:** minor
**Area:** log-schema

**Issue:** The schema version is `"1.0"` (`MAJOR.MINOR`), not `"1.0.0"` (full SemVer with PATCH). This is a deliberate two-tuple shape (per the docstring: "MAJOR bump = breaking; MINOR bump = additive"). It is internally consistent and the parser enforces exactly two parts.

The risk is purely external-perception: every reader who sees `cdd_log_version: "1.0"` in a log will mentally parse it as SemVer and may either (a) be confused by missing PATCH, or (b) write tooling that splits on `.` expecting three parts. The CDD-internal docstring is clear; downstream tooling (replay validators, log-archival tools) will not read the docstring.

**Why it matters:** Once logs ship with `"1.0"`, switching to `"1.0.0"` later is a same-MAJOR-different-shape migration — `_parse_schema_version` now requires exactly two parts and would reject `"1.0.0"`. Reversing is cheap pre-v0.2.0; expensive after.

**Recommended fix:** Either (a) declare in the docstring that "PATCH is intentionally absent — schema versions are MAJOR.MINOR only, not full SemVer," or (b) extend `_parse_schema_version` to accept and ignore PATCH (tolerant read of `"1.0.0"` as `(1, 0)`). Option (a) is the smaller move and lines up with the existing two-tuple semantics. I'd ship (a) as a one-line docstring tweak in the v0.1.0 fix-fold; not a blocker.

The `stacklevel=3` on `warnings.warn` is correct (caller of `read_log` sees the warning at their call site, not inside `from_dict` → `_parse_schema_version`).

## Roadmap coherence check

The 10-sprint plan reads cleanly. Pre-alpha now contains S1–S4 (bootstrap, core abstractions, protocol hardening, first AI generator) — that's the right grouping because every sprint up through S4 is pre-CLI and breaks freely. Alpha (S5–S7) is CLI + format scaling + first dogfood, which is where external pressure first lands. Beta (S8–S10) is determinism enforcement, hardening, public release — also right. The deferred-finding tickets are placed where their consumer first appears: F1/F3/F4/F5/F9 in S3 (protocol shape pressure), F7/F11 in S4 (first real generator), F8 in S6 (large logs), F10 in S8 (replay validation), F14 in S9 (API freeze), F16 in S10 (public docs). Each placement is defensible. The one thing the roadmap doesn't yet show but should is that S3 (v0.1.5) explicitly bumps `_LOG_SCHEMA_VERSION` to `"1.1"` — `parent_session_id` was added in v0.1.0 as a MINOR addition relative to a never-shipped prior shape, but S3-1's `GenerationRequest` and S3-3's `negotiated_determinism_tier` are clearly minor-bump-worthy schema fields. Not a blocker; flag for the S3 sprint planner.

## Verdict reasoning

All four fix-now items land correctly with proper test coverage (parent_session_id round-trip, schema-version major/minor matrix, naming convention documented, private schema constant). Twelve deferrals are routed to sprints where they pay rent — none are buried in "someday" and none are scheduled too late to avoid bake-in costs. The v0.1.5 insert is the structurally important move: it pulls every protocol-shape finding into one pre-external-consumer sprint, which is the cheapest possible moment to make those changes. v0.1.0 represents a coherent methodology slice: the chain invariants are real, the YAML is round-trippable, the schema is forward-tolerant within MAJOR, and the lineage hook exists to receive future revision sessions. The single delta nit (N1, "1.0" vs "1.0.0") is a docstring-tightening, not a redesign. Tag and ship.
